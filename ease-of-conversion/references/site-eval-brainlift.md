# Site Evaluator — Building Conversion Feasibility (Group E)

## Core Strategic Insights

**Egress Infrastructure > Fire Protection**: Existing egress (corridor widths, exit separation, travel distances) is the primary conversion predictor. Sprinkler retrofits cost $3â€“6/sq ft; egress reconfiguration costs 10â€“20x more and may be structurally impossible.

**Occupancy Classification Hierarchy**: Group B (Business) and A-3 (Assembly) share fundamental DNA with Group E—designed for high-density public occupancy. Group S (Storage) and R (Residential) have incompatible infrastructure patterns.

**12,000 sq ft Threshold**: IBC 903.2.3 triggers mandatory sprinklers at 12,000 sq ft fire area OR any portion below exit discharge. Seek buildings <12K or already sprinklered to eliminate largest variable cost.

**50 Occupants = Real Threshold**: At 50+ occupants, triggers: fire alarm systems, panic hardware on all egress doors, minimum 2 exits, storm shelters (tornado zones). Any serious educational facility exceeds this—design for it from start.

## Fire Protection Requirements

### Sprinkler Systems (IBC 903.2.3)
**Required when EITHER:**
1. Group E fire area exceeds 12,000 sq ft
2. Any portion below lowest level of exit discharge

**Exception**: Below-grade exemption only when every classroom has direct exterior exit at ground level.

**Cost**: $3â€“6 per square foot for retrofit installation (NFPA 13 compliant).

**QC**: Document fire area calculation and basement/below-grade status in first data collection phase. No ambiguity acceptable.

### Fire Alarm Systems (IBC 907.2.3)
**Required**: Group E with occupant load 50+

**Manual pull stations may be omitted when**:
- Building has automatic sprinklers
- Emergency voice/alarm activates on sprinkler waterflow
- Manual activation available from normally occupied location

## Means of Egress Requirements

### Occupant Load Calculation (IBC Table 1004.5)
- **Classrooms**: 20 net sq ft per person
- **Shops/vocational**: 50 net sq ft per person

### Number of Exits (IBC Table 1006.2.1)
- 1â€“49 occupants: 1 exit (with restrictions)
- 50â€“500 occupants: 2 exits
- 501â€“1,000 occupants: 3 exits
- 1,000+ occupants: 4 exits

### Travel Distance (IBC Table 1017.2)
- **Non-sprinklered**: 200 ft maximum
- **Sprinklered**: 250 ft maximum

**QC Gate**: If existing layout exceeds limits and adding exits is not feasible, building fails conversion feasibility.

### Panic Hardware (IBC 1010.2.9)
**Required**: All egress doors from Group E spaces with 50+ occupants

**Specifications**:
- Release with ≤15 lbs force
- Actuator ≥50% of door width
- Mounted 34â€“48 inches above floor

**Cost**: $300â€“500 per door (typical facilities have 4â€“8 doors = $1,200â€“4,000 total)

## Construction Type Constraints (IBC Chapter 5)

### Allowable Height and Area (Tables 504.3, 504.4, 506.2)
Construction type (I-A through V-B) determines max height and floor area based on fire-resistance.

**Sprinkler Bonuses**:
- Single-story: Up to 300% area increase
- Multi-story: Up to 200% area increase
- Height: +1 story and +20 feet

**Action**: Identify construction type from permit records. Cross-reference against IBC Tables to verify existing building doesn't exceed allowable limits for Group E.

**Special Requirement**: Unsprinklered Group E in Type II-B, III-B, IV, or V-B requires minimum 1-hour fire-resistance rating for floor above usable basement (IBC 510.2).

---

## Additional Requirements

### Storm Shelters (IBC 423.5)
**Required**: 250 mph tornado design wind zones, Group E with 50+ occupants

**Capacity**: Greater of total classroom/office occupant load OR largest assembly space

**Max travel**: 1,000 ft from building exterior door to shelter

### Acoustics (IBC 1208.2)
Enhanced acoustics required in classrooms ≤20,000 cubic feet (ICC A117.1 Section 808)

### Carbon Monoxide (IBC 915.2.3)
CO detectors required in classrooms with automatic transmission to monitored location

## Rapid Feasibility Screening Protocol

**Objective**: Identify fatal flaws before detailed analysis

**Actions**:
1. Identify original occupancy classification → Assign difficulty tier
2. Confirm construction type → Check height/area limits
3. Calculate fire area + check for below-grade → Determine sprinkler requirement (YES/NO/UNKNOWN)
4. Measure travel distances → Compare to 200 ft (non-sprinkler) or 250 ft (sprinkler)
5. Count exits + verify separation → Check against occupant load requirements
6. Document existing fire protection → Identify upgrade needs

**Definition of Done**:
- Original occupancy documented
- Construction type confirmed
- Fire area calculated
- Sprinkler requirement status: YES/NO/UNKNOWN
- Travel distance status: COMPLIANT/NON-COMPLIANT/UNKNOWN
- Preliminary feasibility score: GREEN/YELLOW/ORANGE/RED
