# Brainlift: Microschool Capacity Rules

Owner: JC Fischer

## Core Principle

A microschool's capacity is determined by one question: can every student be seated, surfaced, and supervised simultaneously during the 2-hour core learning block? If the answer is no for even one student, that student does not exist in the capacity model.

## Levels

There are 5 levels in Alpha School; microschools typically use the first 4:

| Level | Grades | Base SF/Student | Guide Ratio |
|-------|--------|-----------------|-------------|
| LL | K-1 | 42 SF | 1:10 |
| L1 | 2nd-3rd | 42 SF | 1:10 |
| L2 | 4th-5th | 42 SF | 1:12 |
| MS | 6th-8th | 42 SF | 1:12 |
| HS | 9th-12th | N/A (not typically in microschools) | — |

## Rule 1: Gross SF Ceiling (Hard Cap)

No building can exceed 1 student per 100 gross SF. This is not a target — it is a hard ceiling.

- Formula: floor(NFA / 100)
- NFA = Net Floor Area (total usable interior space)
- This is always checked first and cannot be exceeded regardless of how much NLA exists

## Rule 2: 80% NLA Cap

No more than 80% of the building's floor plan can be used as Net Learning Area. A minimum of 20% is needed for non-learning uses, even if 100% of the floor area appears to qualify as NLA.

- Formula: Usable NLA = min(actual NLA, NFA × 0.80)

## Rule 3: Net Learning Area (NLA) Definition

NLA is defined as an area where students are provided with a dedicated seat for the morning block of core learning. Must have space for a simultaneous morning block where every student has:
- A seat
- A work surface
- Continuous Guide sightlines

### NLA Qualification Requirements
- Must have line of sight of sunlight (natural light)
- Must be within 200 ft of a bathroom
- LL and L1 NLA require 1 guide per room minimum
- L2 and MS NLA may span multiple adjacent rooms or open offices, as long as the Guide can maintain visual contact with all students
- Rooms without windows CANNOT be NLA — they should be prioritized as workshop spaces

## Rule 4: Classroom Configuration & Sharing Multiplier

When more grade levels must share fewer rooms, effective density goes up because furniture is required to create visual and audio separation between levels.

| Classrooms | Configuration | Levels per Room | Multiplier | Effective SF/Student |
|------------|---------------|-----------------|------------|---------------------|
| 4+ | Each level gets own room | 1 | 1.00x | 42 SF |
| 3 | MS/L2 share, L1, LL | 2 (one pair) | 1.10x | 46 SF |
| 2 | MS/L2 share, LL/L1 share | 2 (two pairs) | 1.10x | 46 SF |
| 1 | All levels share | 3-4 | 1.35x-1.50x | 57-63 SF |

A "classroom" for this purpose is a room large enough to seat a group of students (generally ≥200 SF).

## Rule 5: Preliminary Capacity

Preliminary capacity = the LOWER of:
- Gross SF ceiling (NFA / 100)
- NLA capacity (usable NLA / effective SF per student)

## Rule 6: Workshop Requirement

Minimum 1 workshop room per microschool. Two options:

**Hybrid option (preferred):** A core learning room can double as workshop if it exceeds the required space for the number of students by 20%, to create room for furniture that changes the space between purposes.

**Dedicated room option:** One shared room at 35 SF × students per guide (e.g., 10 students/guide = 350 SF room).

Rooms without windows should be prioritized as workshop spaces since they cannot be NLA.

If neither option provides a usable workshop solution, reduce capacity until a solution succeeds.

## Rule 7: 1:1 Meeting Space ("Limitless")

At least one audio-separated space for Guide 1:1 meetings. Minimum: a 100 SF room or a 2-person pod.

## Rule 8: Play Area (Required)

**On-site passes if:**
- At least 100 SF of green area per student, OR
- A decent playscape is present (dedicated, safe, functional, age-appropriate play structure)

**Off-site passes only if:**
- Park or play area within a 5-minute walk for a 7-year-old
- Reached with no major street crossings (maximum one crosswalk)
- Safe, maintained public space

Major street = more than one lane in each direction, or a signalized arterial road.

## Rule 9: Dining / Commons

Must be in addition to core learning space (but can be used for workshops).
- 15 SF per student for simultaneous dining of 50% of capacity
- Include 10% staff uplift
- Formula: dining SF needed = ceil(capacity × 0.50 × 1.10) × 15

## Rule 10: Other Required Spaces

None of these count as NLA or Workshop:

| Space | Minimum Size |
|-------|-------------|
| Lobby | 200 SF |
| Security Vestibule | 120 SF (optional) |
| Focus rooms | 70 SF |
| IT Closet | 50 SF |
| Janitorial | 50 SF per floor |
| Bathrooms | Every floor; child-height for LL/L1; same floor or within 200 ft for younger students |
| Kitchen | 200 SF (warming); optional — keep if already in building |

## Rule 11: Grade Span Reduction

Start with K-8 (4 levels). If the building cannot support K-8:
1. Remove the oldest level (MS) and retest
2. Continue removing top levels until the site can sustain at least 20 students
3. If it cannot sustain 20 students at any grade span, the site fails

## Final Capacity

Final capacity is the HIGHEST student count that SIMULTANEOUSLY satisfies ALL of the following:
1. 100 gross SF per student (hard ceiling)
2. Adjusted NLA per student (including sharing multiplier)
3. LL/L1 single-room minimum size
4. L2+ visual contact rule
5. Workshop requirement (hybrid or dedicated)
6. 1:1 meeting space
7. Play area requirement

If any rule fails, the site fails — unless leadership explicitly approves an exception.
